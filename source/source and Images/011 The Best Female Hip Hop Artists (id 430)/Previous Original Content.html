Since the uprising of hip hop, the industry has been male-dominated. However, times and seasons have changed to receive more female artists in this genre. Many female hip-hop artists are doing a great job and uplifting the industry.

Young M.A.

The name Young M.A. is the stage name of one Katorah Hortense Marrero. She was born on April 2, 1992, in New York and is a mix of a Jamaican mother and Puerto Rican father.

She first shook the hip hop industry with her hit song "Ooouuu." Her songs mainly revolve around acquisitions, violence, sexuality, and wealth.

Her music videos tell it all, and her personality is interesting for the role play. Young M.A. has made her stand as a lesbian since the commencing of her career.

Tierra Whack

Tierra Helena Whack is a young lady born on August 5, 1995, in North Philadelphia.

She is the product of a single-mother upbringing and specializes in rap music, whose interest she developed as a child with Dr. Seuss's material. She explained in one of her interviews how she would rhyme her sentences for her homework.

Despite relocating to Atlanta with her family, she returned to Philadelphia years later as she felt it was best for her career, and it was. She met Kenete Simms, who taught her how to use the equipment and mix music more easily. Critics loved her track album "Whack World," a compilation of short songs, each less than a minute.

Solange

Solange Piaget Knowles was born on June 24, 1986.

She is a woman of many genres, including hip hop, funk, pop, and R&B. She is the young sister to the iconic Beyoncé Knowles, who we will discuss later.

Her relevance to hip hop is mainly connected to her endorsement deal with a young children's merchandising line. Her affiliation with hip-hop music is largely influenced her son's love for hip-hop music.

She began to promote a toy-line for preschoolers, Baby Jamz, and later became the executive producer of toys with hip-hop-inspired nursery rhymes.

Rye Rye

Ryeisha Berrain was born on November 25, 1990. She began her music career at 16 years and attributed her passion to her love for poetry and stories, which intensified this desire.

Her sister's friend, Blaqstarr, took her up and worked with her until her 2006 hit "Shake it to the Ground." Blaqstarr connected Rye Rye with M.I.A. and Diplo, although she did not know who they were in all their popularity.

The proceeding road tours that Rye tagged along with M.I.A. did her career in her favor as she did the opening song for each of them. She even appeared in M.I.A.'s music video for "Paper Planes."

Rihanna

Her real name is Robyn Rihanna Fenty, and she was born on February 20, 1988, at Saint Michael; however, she was raised in Bridgetown, Barbados.

Rihanna's music experience covers various genres, including hip hop, pop, reggae, and E.D.M. This article focuses on her star in hip-hop music.

She is categorized as one of the most successful female artists of all time. As of 2019, she was the wealthiest female artist with a net worth of $600 million.

Rihanna has collaborated with some successful music artists like Drake in producing hit songs.

Queen Latifah

Queen Latifah's real name is Dana Elaine Owens. She was born on March 18, 1970,in Newark, New Jersey.

The beginning of her career was beatboxing for a ladies hip hop group, Ladies Fresh. DJ King Gemini made a demo recording of Latifah rapping and shared it; eventually, it got to Dante Ross, who signed Latifah in 1989 and issued her first single,"Wrath of My Madness," which was sampled in other artists' songs.

Queen Latifah was recognized as the few artists with a contralto, the ability to sing and rap well. She is written in hip-hop history as one of the most influential female pioneers.

Nicki Minaj

Nicki's real name is Onika Tanya Maraj-Petty; she was born on December 8, 1982, at Saint James in the Port of Spain. However, she was raised in the Queens borough of New York City. This beauty is a mix of Trinidadian and American blood.

Minaj does many things, but she is best known for her rapping. It is impossible to hear any of her songs without getting a treat for her rapping skills.

There is a touch of hip hop in every song she does, including the mixed genres. Some examples include "Right Through Me," "Bed of Lies," "Pills and Portions," and "High School."

Nicki remains one of the best female artists in the music industry, hip hop notwithstanding.

Megan Thee Stallion

Megan Jovon Ruth Pete is the real name of Megan Thee Stallion. She was born on February 15, 1995, in San Antonio, Texas. However, she was raised in Houston by her mother.

Megan's popularity began when videos of her freestyling did rounds on various platforms like Instagram. She was exposed to rapping from an early age through her mother, who carried her to recording sessions.

Pete was in university when she began uploading her freestyling videos on social media. A video of her rap battle with males at a cypher went viral as she did tremendously well. Since then, she rose to popularity.

She has collaborated with famous rappers like Young Thug and is the proud owner of the song "Savage" that hit the media as a challenge on sites like TikTok.

Mary J. Blidge

Mary Jane Blidge was born on January 11, 1971, in the Bronx borough in New York City.

Her childhood was rough, with traumatic events like sexual molestation and her parents' divorce since she was five years old, driving her down an uncertain path.

Her mother's boyfriend shared her recording of Anita Baker's cover of "Caught Up in the Rapture" with Jeff Redd; this eventually got her a backup vocalist role at the Andre Harrell record label as the youngest and first female artist.

Blidge's songs were largely influenced by her previous experiences and blessed the heart of many fans. Her legacy leaves on to date, and she is still delivering good pieces.

M.I.A

Mathangi "Maya" Arulpragasam is an English rapper born on18th July 1975 in Hounslow, London. Her family relocated to northern Sri Lanka when she was six months old, and the rest of her 11 years of life leading up to their settlement in London was filled with warfare and fleeing from warzones.

Although she is listed among the best hip hop artists, she knows how to blend various music genre elements to produce a harmonious piece. Her songs bear political and philosophical commentaries most of the time. She is an intentional activist that knows just how to deliver the message to the people by what they like, music. Is it possible that Mathangi's life contributed to her activism?

Her recording career began in 2002 before she rose to fame with two singles, "Sunshowers" and "Galang."

Lil' Mama

Lil' Mama, the stage name forNiatia Jessica Kirkland,was born on October 4, 1989, in Brooklyn, New York. In many cultures, the firstborn daughter is equivalent to the mother of the house. Since Jessica was the first daughter in a family of eight siblings, she was Lil' Mama.

Her love for rap began with a preference for music and poetry. Some of the influences around her life were her D.J. father and singer mother. Her role models were Lauryn Hill, Missy Elliot, and Mc Lyte.

The young generation connected with her as they saw her as a voice to their issues. She took a societal role that closely resembled her Lil' Mama role in the family.

Lauryn Hill

Lauryn Noelle Hill was born on May 26, 1975,in Newark, New Jersey, and was one of the earliest female rappers in hip hop.

She broke the boundaries barring females from rapping, but she also introduced a new style, melodic rapping.

Hill's indulgence in rapping was motivated by her exploration of African-American and Caribbean music, blended into other genres.

She was part of a popular band named Fugees back in the 90s but moved on to work on her solo album in 1997 after the band's split following her romanticism with Jean, her recruiter's cousin. Her solo album was named "The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill."

Charli Baltimore

Azealia Banks

Azealia Amanda Banks was born on May 31, 1991,in Manhattan borough in New York. She was raised in Harlem.

An abusive mother raised her because her father died while she was young. At 14 years, she went to live with her sister.

Her first result was acting, but she missed the cut for three major auditions with Nickelodeon, T.B.S., and Law & Order. She wrote rap and R&B songs as an outlet for her feelings and dropped out of high school to pursue music as a full-time career.

Her other popular name is Miss Banks which she used when releasing some of her songs, like her debut recording "Gimme a Chance."

She identifies as bisexual and is open about it.
