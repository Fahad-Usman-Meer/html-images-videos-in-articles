A Digital Audio Workstation or a DAW is the software program that musicians use for producing music.

In today's world, DAWs have become a necessity for music creation as they're more accessible than ever before. Moreover, they are only getting better and more powerful.

Whether you have just started creating a song or you're about to reach the final mastering step, you will require music recording software to turn your vision into a reality.

Now there are several amazing DAWs in the market today, so how do you pick one from the sea of amazing options out there? Don't worry, in this article, you will get to know about some of the best DAWs out there in the musical realm.

This guide will let you know everything you need to know to pick out the best DAW for you. But before that, let's understand the basics.

What is a Digital Audio Workstation?

A DAW or a Digital Audio Workstation is a software program that is used for composing, recording, producing, editing, and mixing audio and MIDI.

The DAWs will facilitate the mixing of multiple sound sources on time-based grids. There are several amazing DAWs in the market, each with its different strengths and weaknesses.

How do you choose the best from the rest?

There are some important points that you must consider as you choose the DAW you will be using to create your music.

The first step would be that despite different workflows and looks, they essentially do the same thing – help you produce music.

Once you've grasped the basic metaphors of digital music production, you might feel that your choice of DAW has become a little less important. The primary difference between the DAWs will be about workflow and features.

The only to select the best DAW for you would be by testing them out and finding the one that fits your needs the best.

Download a free demo

Most DAWs today offer free trials to help you decide if that DAW is what you're looking for. Once you have honed in on the DAWs that you want to try, download their demo versions and see how you feel during practice sessions.

In case you're just getting started with the basic ideas of DAW, you might consider trying a free DAW demo or even a free full version.

There are many great DAW programs out there that are completely free. While they might not have all the functions that professionals rely on, but they'll certainly help you produce great music.

Starting slow and going for a free version or a demo version first will help you save time and money when it comes to making a commitment.

Best Digital Audio Workstations for creating music

Now that you know how to find the right DAW for you, it's time to check out some of the best options available in the market.

Here is a list of 10 of the best DAWS (in no particular order) available in the market. Go through them and select the one that suits you the best.

Bitwig

Bitwig Studio is an exceptional DAW launched in 2014 by a group of former Ableton developers.

Bitwig will draw inspiration from Ableton though it has pioneered its own composition approaches and creative workflows. Strong hardware integration, songwriting-friendly tools, and unique modulation options make this DAW a solid option for any music creator.

Moreover, Bitwig is very easy to learn, especially if you're starting out with recording software and it'll give you plenty of room to grow.

Ableton Live 10

Ableton Live is easily one of the biggest players on the DAW scene and for good reason. For years Ableton has been helping music creators with its intuitive session view and an inspiring workflow, making it one of the favorites among producers, especially the ones who produce electronic music.

Ableton's synthesis plugins and built-in sampling are sound-design tools of extremely high quality.

The composition workflow in Ableton Live 10 is among the easiest and one of the best to get quick results. The session view will allow you to jam your arrangements by trigger clips and loops in different combinations.

Moreover, Ableton continues to release consistently excellent sample packs to add to its already huge library of content that you can use in your tracks.

Since successfully acquiring digital audio legend Cycling'74 in 2017, the company has been able to offer an insanely flexible Max digital signal processing environment Live as Max4Live. This makes Live one of the most versatile sound design tools out there.

Studio One 4.5

Studio One was Presonus' entry to the DAW scene.

It is relatively newer than most other DAWs but it really came into its own with the Studio One v4.5. Version 4.5 boasts some of the most uniquely powerful tools that you will struggle to find in other DAWs.

The proprietary high-resolution internal MIDI protocol will allow for far smoother parameter changes in the MIDI parts while the dedicated hardware interface plugin will allow you to use the outboard gear in an easier-than-ever way.

The workflow in this DAW is focused on creativity and inspiration.

The ‘songwriting first' design of the DAW will make it very easy to compose fast and you could even output printable scores and lead sheets from the parts that you create in the DAW.

While Studio One 4.5 might seem like a bit of an underdog, but this gives it a lot of room for innovation.

Pro Tools 2020

Pro Tools is the ultimate industry standard DAW. You will find Pro Tools being used in almost every professional recording studio. This DAW was designed for traditional recording in a professional studio setting and it certainly excels at every part of that process.

Professional engineers absolutely love it for the high-quality mixing environment and the speed of editing it offers.

While it might have a steeper learning curve than most other DAWs but it is definitely worth putting in the time in case you ever hope of working in a professional studio. Pro Tools comes in many configurations, including the free Pro Tools First introductory edition, which is limited to 16 tracks.

The premium, hardware-accelerated version is called Pro Tools Ultimate while the standard native version is known as ‘Pro Tools'.

Similar to other paid software, Pro Tools has slowly transitioned to a hybrid license/subscription model with each edition named after the year of its release (the latest version is Pro Tools 2020).

Pro Tools will also require you to use the iLok hardware DRM platform, which might be off putting for some users.

Audacity

Released in 2009, Audacity is free recording software that is loved by hundreds of thousands of music creators.

Audacity is compatible with almost every operating system while it is very easy to download right away. This software has everything that you'll require for recording audio on a timeline with no extra features.

It doesn't record MIDI, which is why using virtual instruments such as VST synths is out of questions whereas plugin effects will have to be applied destructively offline.

This means that Audacity might not be the best choice if you're looking for a full mix.

However, in case you're just getting started with the basic ideas of digital recording, this free tool might be the perfect way to jump in.

Logic Pro X

The superb Logic Pro X is Apple's premium audio software profit.

Logic Pro X shares its user-friendly design philosophy with Garageband. If you decided to get started with Garageband, you will have no problem using Logic Pro X.

In the recent versions, Apple has focused more on creating exceptional built-in plugins that'll give the music creators a suite of tools that they'll need to have great recording sessions. Couple that with audio editing, MIDI, and a powerful sample editor, and Logic Pro X has managed to strike the perfect balance between ease of use and functionality.

Moreover, Logic has even decided to add a number of pro-level features such as timing correction, pitch, and convincing guitar amp emulation plugins. Generally, you would have had to pay hundreds or sometimes thousands for this kind of functionality from third parties.

The huge amount of samples and plugins bundled with Logic is truly impressive. If you are in the market for a one-stop shot DAW, then Logic Pro X should be your first choice.

Reaper

Reaper (an acronym for Rapid Environment for Audio Production, Engineering, and Recording), is developed by the developers of the WinAmp audio player and the Gnutella peer-to-peer network.

It is a powerful, adaptive, and comprehensive DAW without the need for any engineering compromises. The DAW comes with a free 60-day trial period with the individual license only costing $60 USD, making it one of the best DAW programs that you will find a great investment in the long-run.

There aren't any track types in Reaper, as each track that you create will be able to do anything you want it to (audio, video, MIDI, bussing), which makes arranging very simple.

Steinberg Cubase Pro 10

Steinberg Cubase was among the first few commercially available DAWs, which is why it still has such a massive following.

Cubase Pro 10 is packed with numerous latest features that you'll need to create a professional-quality track.

Cubase started out as a MIDI-only application with its MIDI editing capabilities still among the strongest. Its mixing and audio features are no slouch either as this DAW can do things that only the big names are capable of.

Steinberg has successfully pioneered the popular VST plugin format, which means that it has great compatibility with free VST plugins, among others, especially on Windows.

Cubase comes with a ton of great features, which makes it a worthy consideration if you're looking for the best DAW in the market.

Garageband

At this point, Garageband has almost become a household name for musicians. This free DAW developed by Apple has done far more for democratizing music production than any music software.

In fact, you might even be surprised to know the names of hit records that have been produced on it, considering it is a free program that comes with every version of Mac OSX.

Moreover, Garageband provides several helpful tips in-app that are written in simple language that is accessible to musicians of all production skill levels. This helps you learn something you might not have known before.

Overall, this free DAW is a simple yet effective tool to help you get started with music production. If you're reading this on your Mac, you know what to do.

So start making exciting music from today and see how quickly Garageband helps you produce amazing music.

FL Studio 20

FL Studio or previously known as Fruity Loops is a favorite among hip-hop and electronic music producers as it has a no-nonsense interface that'll get you started making beats right away.

Most beatmakers get their first taste of creating grooves and loops with FL studio and continue doing so as their career progresses.

The FL Studio v20 includes a mobile extension of the DAW, extensive integration with AKAI's FL Studio specific hardware, and a lot more to help you in creating music. The DAW comes with a wide variety of native plugins including samplers, synths, and virtual effect units.

Moreover, Image-Line is offering free lifetime updates to the users, which means if you purchase this program once, you will have access to every version of FL Studio from now onwards.
