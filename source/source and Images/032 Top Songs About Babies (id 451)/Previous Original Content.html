The life-changing connection between a parent and their baby is one that is seen as inspired art for centuries. Musicians from every side of the proverbial block have used this over the years to produce great hits. If you're a lucky parent who is about to be blessed with a newborn, there are tons of beautiful, heartbreaking, and emotionally thumping hits over the years that you can listen to and hype yourself up to be a parent.

So for the new parents, here is a great playlist of some of the top songs about babies that you need to listen to at all costs. Stop what you're doing, find a quiet spot, forget all the problems in the world, and get ready to be inspired by some of the biggest artists in the industry as they sing to you about babies and being parents.

I Saw God Today by George Strait

If you're a parent or if you've seen the miracle of childbirth, then you'll understand this 2008 country hit.

After being with his beloved wife for 18 hours of labor, the father goes to a coffee shop to take a break. He notices that he is seeing the world differently after the birth of his baby girl, thinking to himself that the fingerprints of God are everywhere.

With Arms Wide Open by Creed

Upon learning that he will be having a son, the narrator in this hit 2000 rock ballad is awe-struck. He is not able to believe that he and his partner have created a life together.

Prayers, tears of joy, and happiness begin to flow, as the narrator imagines all of the things he'll show his son.

The future father also hopes that his son would be curious about the world and greet it with open arms.

Papa Don't Preach by Madonna

In this beautiful 1986 pop hit, a troubled pregnant teen approaches her dad to inform him of her pregnancy.

He had warned her before about the guy she was seeing, but she got pregnant and she has decided to keep the baby. What she seeks now is advice from her father but what she gets from him is constant preaching and second-guessing.

Isn't She Lovely by Stevie Wonder

Not even a minute old, the darling daughter that legendary Stevie Wonder is singing about in this 1976 pop hit couldn't be any more divine.

As if truly knowing love for the first time, the new father in this song is in awe of his everyday miracle.

Babyfather by Sade

In this reggae-infused 2010 pop song, a pregnant woman is talking to her unborn child.

She describes to her new child how she and the father met and how much love they have for the baby they've created together.

All-American Girl by Carrie Underwood

Some parents have a strong preference for a baby of one sex or the other. This 2007 country hit features a husband who dreams of having a son, but his mind has quickly changed when he sees the birth of his infant daughter.

The song's tune describes how this girl grows up to marry and have her own child. Moreover, the husband was smitten by her to the point that he actually preferred to have a little girl who resembled his lovely wife.

A Mother's Prayer by Celine Dion

This stunning 2004 classical crossover song is a lyrically adapted version of the duet that Dion sang with Italian singer Andrea Bocelli.

This song describes a mother's requests to God to guide her daughter and help here succeed in her life path. Moreover, she is asking him to keep the child safe and remind her constantly that she is loved.

Miracle by Foo Fighters

This 2005 hit will resonate with new parents who have to get up every two hours to feed their newborn and what it feels like being blurry-eyed because of lack of sleep and protectively holding their precious miracle while also 'begging for sweet relief'.

It Takes A Man by Chris Young

The young, unmarried couple in this 2008 country fit are facing a moment of truth as they're parked in the driveway of the lady's family home. She shares the news that she is pregnant, which she will be keeping with or without his support.

Hoping that he would offer words of reassurance and decide to stay, she feels crestfallen by his silence.

It Won't Be Like This For Long by Darius Rucker

The exhausted dad in this country song from 2008 has not been able to sleep by his newborn constantly crying throughout the night but he also reassures his wife that it wouldn't be like this for long.

As the daughter grows into a pre-schooler and later a teenager, she brings different challenges, and at each point, offers that familiar, bittersweet refrain.

You're Gonna Be by Reba McEntire

In this sweet, melodious 2005 country hit, the narrator is talking to her newborn baby. In awe of getting the responsibility she has now, she explains how life isn't fair all the time and she doesn't have the answers to all the problems.

However, she will always be there for her child to provide her support and unconditional love.

Haven't Met You Yet by Michael Bublé

Technically, this jazzy 2009 pop ditty is more about waiting for a love relationship, but the lyrics and spirit in the song are appropriate for parents who are awaiting the birth of their little one.

This song also describes disappointments and past heartbreaks that are offset by the optimism of anticipating the future meeting of someone who will make everything worthwhile.

Three Hearts by Alex Clare

The narrator in this 2014 rock hit has found his lucky charm, the love of his life, his newborn.

Excited about the baby that is about to be born, he describes how he has money for a home for their family and looks forward to having three hearts beating under his roof.

There Goes My Life by Kenny Chesney

When the young man in this hit 2003 country song learns about his impending fatherhood, all that he can think of is 'there goes my life'.

He is so young and feels very ill-equipped to be a parent. As his baby is born and grows into a child and ultimately a young adult, the phrase takes on an entirely new meaning. His whole world is proudly wrapped up in taking care of his unexpected daughter.

Small Bump by Ed Sheeran

Stillbirths, or deaths of babies in utero after 20 weeks of pregnancy account for under 1% of pregnancies but the ones affected by the loss are left devastated for a long time.

In this 2012 pop hit, a grieving father emotionally addresses his stillborn child. He regretfully imagines what his firstborn child would have looked like while also describing the comfort he would have provided the baby if the baby had been born.

Having My Baby by Paul Anka feat Odia Coates

This classic pop duet released in 1974 is probably the one that first comes to your mind when you think of pregnancy ad newborn baby songs, at least that is the case for most people who love old, classic music.

The couple narrating this song sees the surprise pregnancy as the celebration of love. As the father-to-be is complimenting his partner on the beautiful changes that impending motherhood is doing to her body, she is letting him know that she is as enthused about the baby's arrival as he is.

Capri by Colbie Callait

Cradling her growing belly, the expecting mother in this 2007 pop song protects the life that is unfolding inside.

Even though it will be months before she gets to meet her sweet baby, the mother is still cherishing her child as an unborn angel. She eagerly anticipates the arrival of her mini version and thinks about their shared future.

Two Pink Lines by Eric Church

Almost half of all the pregnancies in the US are unintended, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. In this 2006 country hit, the couple definitely did not plan on having a baby when they started fooling around at the start of their relationship.

Just out of school, the couple had just been dating for six weeks. As the girl in this song takes the pregnancy test and they wait for the results, the boyfriend also thinks about how the girl's dad will react if the test comes out positive.

To Zion by Lauryn Hill

The expecting mother in this moving 1998 R&B song is thrilled and in awe of the duty of bringing forth life into this world.

She also feels that she has been entrusted to give birth to an angel and take care of her.

Even though she is naturally torn between a potential family and a career, she decides that the baby is a reflection of God's grace.

Stay Up Late by Talking Heads

Found on 'Little Creatures', this slice of 80s pop from the Talking Heads finds Bryne confused and torn between the glee of his new daughter and the implications of treating her too much like merely a plaything.

It is the carefree mess around even before the early morning wake-up.

Precious by Depeche Mode

Even though Core is known for being highly guarded about his lyrical inspiration, he did make a rare admittance that this 2005 track came after considering the impact his divorce from former wife Suzanne must have had on his kids.

With the several lyrical references made to the fragile, brittle objects, the track's stark honestly certainly cuts through the band's sheen in an affecting, self-deprecating triumph.

Just The Two Of Us by Will Smith

While it was originally written by Bill Withers and Grover Washington Jr. as a love song, Will Smith re-imagined its romantic skin into the body of his charmingly distinctively 90s jester character, to deliver a jolly ode about the loving relationship between father and son.

If you have felt out of touch with the changing times, you can take consolation in Smith's pains with "101 Dalmatians on CD-ROM" being too much for him.

Make sure you avoid watching this song if you have cute little kids running around the house.

A Little Soul by Pulp

Even though not strictly written for his kids, 'A Little Soul' was inspired by Cocker's experiences with his dad when he was a little child. As with most of Pulp's best songs, the track hides a dark edge behind a catchy, breezy melody.

Having been abandoned by his dad Mac, who left for Australia when Cocker was just 7, it is a highly heartbreaking song on how a parent could simultaneously be a father and a stranger.

The pain and pressure experienced by Cocker are imagined with a few sensitive, subtle touches in the track's video and released in June 1998 on 'This Is Hardcore'.

Beautiful Boy by John Lennon

Julian, Sean's elder half-brother, had some of his dad's most well-known songs written for him – 'Lucky in the Sky with Diamonds' was allegedly about one of his nursery school drawings, and 'Hey Jude', initially 'Hey Jules', was a cheer-up song from McCartney in the midst of his parents' divorce.

However, this track was written for Lennon's only kid with Yoko Ono and is a woozy psychedelic lullaby in its dreamy quality and tender in its message. The whisper closing the track might be a tad bit creepy if it wasn't for the steel drums backing it.

Havin' a baby by R Kelly

There are tons of songs about newfound fatherhood but very few of them describe the process in quite as much detail as 'Havin' a Baby' by R Kelly.

R Kelly's ever so literal depiction of the arrival of his new son, Robert Jr. In the song, R Kelly will hold the listener's hand and take them on a journey, right from the moment he got the news of pregnancy all the way to his step by step narration of his newborn's escape from the womb.
